# Your First Week with Genezio

*A practical day-by-day guide for Digital Marketing Managers. By the end of your first week, you'll have your baseline scores, your first competitor analysis, and a prioritized content action list.*

> **Before you start:** Have your brand name, top 2–3 competitor names, and a list of 5–10 topics you want to rank for in AI answers ready. Topics should be category-level phrases like "AI visibility platform," "project management for remote teams," or "CRM for startups" — not product names.

---

## Day 1 — Monday: Set Up Your Brand and Baseline

*~2 hours · Goal: first conversations running by end of day*

- [ ] **Add your brand** in Genezio. Include your website URL and a short description of what you do. → *Core Concepts → Brands*
- [ ] **Add your top 2–3 competitors.** Genezio tracks them in parallel so every score you see already has a comparative layer. → *Core Concepts → Competitors*
- [ ] **Create your first 3–5 Topics.** Start narrow — one topic per core use case or buyer persona. You can expand later. → *Core Concepts → Topics*
- [ ] **Create 2–3 Scenarios per topic.** Write them as real user questions — the way a buyer would actually phrase it to ChatGPT. Not "CRM features" but "What CRM should a 10-person startup use?" → *Running Conversations → Creating Scenarios*
- [ ] **Select your LLM engines.** Start with ChatGPT and Perplexity. Add Claude and Gemini once you have the baseline. → *Running Conversations → Selecting LLM Engines*
- [ ] **Run your first conversations.** Results typically available within minutes.

**End of Day 1 deliverable:** First AI Visibility % numbers for your brand and competitors. Initial list of which sources AI is already citing in your category.

---

## Day 2 — Tuesday: Read Your Baseline Scores

*~1.5 hours · Goal: understand where you stand and why*

- [ ] **Check AI Visibility % and AI Recommendations %** for each topic. Note which topics you score well on and which are gaps.
- [ ] **Open Citations.** For each topic where your score is low, look at which URLs AI is citing. Write them down — this list becomes your PR and link-building roadmap.
- [ ] **Check Statements.** What claims is AI making about your brand? Flag anything outdated, negative, or missing.
- [ ] **Compare your scores with competitors.** Note who leads, by how much, and what sources they're cited from that you aren't.

> **Tip:** Pick the one topic where the gap between you and the top competitor is smallest — that's your first quick win opportunity.

**End of Day 2 deliverable:** Scored list of topics to prioritize. Citation source list (5–10 domains AI trusts in your category). Statement issues flagged.

---

## Day 3 — Wednesday: Deep Dive into Actionable Insights

*~1 hour · Goal: let Genezio tell you exactly what to fix*

- [ ] **Open Actionable Insights.** Read through all four categories: Growth Opportunities, Critical Visibility Gaps, Citation & Authority Leverage, and Positioning & Structural Optimization. → *Actions → Actionable Insights*
- [ ] **Prioritize by impact.** High-priority insights are the ones your competitors are winning right now. Dismiss anything that isn't actionable for your team.
- [ ] **Generate additional insights on demand** for specific topics or scenarios if you want a focused view. You can add custom instructions like "focus on quick wins" or "prioritize opportunities vs. [competitor name]." → *Actions → Actionable Insights → Manually Generating Insights*
- [ ] **Map each insight to a content action:** create content / get a citation / fix schema / earn press coverage.

**End of Day 3 deliverable:** Prioritized list of 5–10 specific actions. Each action mapped to: content type, responsible person, estimated timeline.

---

## Day 4 — Thursday: Build Your First Content Brief

*~2 hours · Goal: first Genezio-informed content piece briefed and assigned*

Take the highest-priority insight from Day 3 and turn it into a proper content brief:

- [ ] **Title and target keyword** — use the exact phrase the AI uses in scenarios where you score low.
- [ ] **Citation sources to reference** — list 3–5 sources from Genezio's Citation section for this topic. Your content should reference these domains as external links.
- [ ] **Statements to counter or reinforce** — include any AI statements about your brand that the content should address.
- [ ] **Schema markup requirements** — flag if the page needs FAQPage, HowTo, or other schema.
- [ ] **Assign and set a re-measurement date** — re-run the relevant Genezio conversations 2–4 weeks after publishing.

**End of Day 4 deliverable:** One complete content brief assigned. Re-measurement date booked in your calendar.

---

## Day 5 — Friday: Set Up Your Monthly Reporting Rhythm

*~1 hour · Goal: AI visibility becomes a permanent part of your marketing metrics*

- [ ] **Screenshot or export your baseline scores** from Day 1. This is your Week 0 benchmark.
- [ ] **Add AI Visibility % to your monthly marketing report.** Track it alongside organic traffic and branded search volume.
- [ ] **Schedule a monthly re-run of all conversations.** Connect content actions to score changes over time.
- [ ] **Add new topics and scenarios** based on what you learned this week.

**End of Week 1 — what you now have:**
- Baseline AI Visibility % and AI Recommendations % across your key topics
- Citation source list — the domains to target for PR and link-building
- Statement audit — what AI says about you today (and what you want it to say)
- Prioritized action list from Actionable Insights
- First content brief assigned
- Monthly reporting rhythm established

---

## Ongoing Monthly Rhythm

| Cadence | Activity | Time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Weekly** | Check Actionable Insights for new recommendations. Brief and assign 1–2 content pieces based on current gaps. | 30 min |
| **Monthly** | Run a full conversation batch. Review score changes. Update your content calendar based on what moved (or didn't). Report AI Visibility % + Share of Voice to leadership. | 2 hours |
| **Quarterly** | Expand topic set. Add new scenarios reflecting seasonal or strategic priorities. Run Comparer Agent analysis on top 2 competitors to check narrative shifts. | Half day |
