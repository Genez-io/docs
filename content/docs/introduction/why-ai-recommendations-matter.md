# Why AI Recommendations Matter

*For CMOs, content strategists, SEO leads, and digital marketing managers.*

> **The one-sentence version:** Genezio measures how often AI assistants like ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity recommend your brand when real people ask about your category — and shows you exactly what to do to get recommended more.

---

## The Metric That Matters Most: AI Recommendations %

When someone asks an AI assistant *"What's the best CRM for a startup of 20 people?"*, the AI responds with a direct answer — usually 3 to 5 recommendations. No list of links. No click required.

If your brand is recommended in that answer, you've won the moment. If it's not, you've lost it — even if you rank #1 on Google for the same query.

**AI Recommendations %** is the percentage of AI conversations where your brand is actively recommended as a solution. This is the single most important number for your marketing team to track.

**AI Visibility %** is the broader measure — how often your brand appears at all (mentioned, cited, or recommended) across AI-generated answers. It gives you the full picture of your brand's AI presence.

| Stat | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **−25%** | Projected decline in traditional search clicks by 2026 due to AI answers |
| **4** | Major AI engines Genezio monitors: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity |
| **2** | Core KPIs: AI Recommendations % and AI Visibility % |

---

## The Old World vs. The New World

| | Traditional Search (SEO) | AI Search (GEO) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **How it works** | User searches → gets links → clicks yours | User asks AI → gets a direct answer → your brand is (or isn't) recommended |
| **What drives results** | Page ranking and click-through rate | Whether your brand is recommended in the AI-generated answer |
| **The risk** | Page 2 gets no traffic | There is no page 2 — you're either recommended or you're not |

> **Key insight:** A brand can rank #1 on Google for "best project management tool" and still be completely absent from ChatGPT's answer to the exact same question. These are two separate games — and most brands are only playing one of them.

## What Genezio Does

Genezio simulates how real users interact with AI assistants, then analyzes what those assistants say about your brand. Think of it as mystery shopping for AI engines — running thousands of conversations automatically.

1. **You define Topics** — the categories you want to be known for
2. **Genezio runs Conversations** — simulated users ask AI assistants real questions
3. **AI answers are analyzed** — who was recommended, mentioned, cited?
4. **You get Insights** — gaps, opportunities, and what to fix

---

## What You Can Do With Genezio

**Measure your brand's AI recommendation rate**
See your AI Recommendations % and AI Visibility % across topics, competitors, and AI engines. Know exactly where you're winning and where you're missing.

**Understand why competitors get recommended instead of you**
Genezio shows which sources AI engines are citing when they recommend competitors. That's your content gap — and your content roadmap.

**Know exactly what content to create**
The Actionable Insights section surfaces specific recommendations: which topics to target, which sources to get cited by, which narratives to build.

**Track improvements over time**
As you publish new content, improve your site, or earn new mentions, your scores update. You can see the direct impact of your marketing work on AI recommendations.

**Benchmark against competitors**
See your Share of Voice — how often your brand is recommended compared to competitors across the same set of AI conversations. A single number your CMO can track every month.

> **For CMOs:** AI Recommendations % and Share of Voice are the two numbers to put on your monthly marketing dashboard alongside organic traffic and branded search volume.
